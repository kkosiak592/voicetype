# Structural Engineering Speech-to-Text Misrecognitions

Comprehensive reference of structural engineering terms commonly misrecognized by speech-to-text models (Whisper, Google Speech, etc.), organized by category. Each entry lists the correct term and its likely misrecognitions.

**Error pattern key:**
- **(H)** = Homophone confusion
- **(P)** = Phonetic similarity / common-word substitution
- **(A)** = Acronym mangling
- **(C)** = Compound/hyphenated term split or merged incorrectly
- **(S)** = Segmentation error (word boundary wrong)

## Implementation Notes for Whisper `initial_prompt`

Based on research, Whisper's `initial_prompt` parameter (max 224 tokens) can bias transcription toward domain-specific vocabulary. Rather than listing terms flat, embed them in natural-sounding context:

```
Structural engineering discussion about AISC steel design. Terms include:
shear, W-section, girder, gusset plate, stiffener, moment connection,
LRFD, ASD, AASHTO, rebar, prestressed, post-tensioning, axial load,
flexure, buckling, diaphragm, splice, ksi, DCR, ETABS, SAP2000.
```

For the corrections dictionary (the `from -> to` mapping in the app), the Priority Dictionary Entries table in Section 10 provides the most impactful entries to seed as defaults.
